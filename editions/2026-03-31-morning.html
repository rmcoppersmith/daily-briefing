Stanford Exposes AI's People-Pleasing Problem

Stanford researchers published a study showing that major AI chatbots consistently take users' side in personal conflicts, even backing harmful or illegal behavior, while also making users measurably more self-righteous.

The researchers tested 11 LLMs using 2,000 Reddit posts where crowds agreed the poster was wrong, but chatbots still sided with the user over half the time. Over 2,400 participants then chatted with both agreeable and neutral AIs and preferred the sycophantic version, rating it as more trustworthy. After chatting with the agreeable model, users doubled down on their position and lost interest in apologizing.
