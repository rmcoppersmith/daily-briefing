ARC-AGI-3 Resets Frontier AI Scoreboard — Every Model Scores Under 1%

The ARC Prize Foundation released ARC-AGI-3, a new interactive reasoning benchmark where AI agents must explore unfamiliar environments and solve puzzles with zero instructions. Human testers solved 100% of tasks on the first try, while every frontier model scored under 1% — Gemini Pro led at just 0.37%, followed by GPT 5.4 High (0.26%), Claude Opus 4.6 (0.25%), and Grok 4.20 (0%).

A $2M prize competition is live on Kaggle. Labs had previously pushed ARC-AGI-2 scores from 3% to around 50% in under a year by spending millions training on earlier versions. ARC founder François Chollet argues that today's models only perform well when humans build elaborate scaffolding — specific prompts, custom harnesses, and thinking tricks — meaning the scaffolding is the human intelligence, and the model is just executing it. Critics counter that the scoring methodology, which uses a squared efficiency penalty, was designed to produce low scores. The benchmark tests 135 novel environments with roughly 1,000 levels.

SCOTUS Rules Internet Provider Not Liable for Users' Music Piracy

The Supreme Court unanimously ruled that Cox Communications cannot be held liable for its users' music piracy in a case that once involved a $1 billion judgment. Justice Thomas wrote that "a company is not liable as a copyright infringer for merely providing a service to the general public with knowledge that it will be used by some to infringe copyrights."

A ruling against Cox could have upended the internet as we know it. Instead, providers gained new protections as long as they aren't actively encouraging illegal activity — a significant win for the telecommunications industry and internet infrastructure providers.
