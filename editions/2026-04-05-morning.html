The scaffold leverages graphene's unique property of being electrically conductive while biocompatible and biodegradable under the right conditions. If clinical trials replicate the animal results, this technology could transform treatment of complex fractures, delayed unions, and non-union injuries that currently require multiple surgeries or lengthy immobilization.

Hubble Constant Measured to 1% Accuracy — 5-Sigma Tension with CMB Remains

A new measurement of the Hubble constant — the rate at which the universe is expanding — has achieved 1% accuracy, the most precise direct measurement to date. The result deepens rather than resolves the Hubble tension: the measured value remains in strong disagreement (5-sigma) with the Hubble constant derived from the cosmic microwave background, suggesting either new physics or unidentified systematic errors.

The Hubble tension has been one of cosmology's most pressing puzzles for a decade. Measurements based on the local universe (using standard candles like Cepheid variable stars and Type Ia supernovae) consistently yield a faster expansion rate (~73 km/s/Mpc) than measurements derived from the early universe's CMB (yielding ~67 km/s/Mpc). At 5-sigma, the discrepancy is well beyond what statisticians attribute to chance. Proposed solutions include: new physics (early dark energy, decaying dark matter, extra neutrino species), systematic errors in the distance ladder, or flaws in the standard cosmological model (ΛCDM). The new 1% measurement tightens the local constraint, making instrument error a less plausible explanation and strengthening the case that the tension reflects genuine new physics.
