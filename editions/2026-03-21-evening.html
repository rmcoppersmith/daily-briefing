UK Brothers Build Robot That Smashes Rubik's Cube World Record

Matthew and Thomas Pidden built a four-armed robot that solved a 4×4 Rubik's Cube in 45.3 seconds, breaking a record that had stood for over a decade. The robot used custom algorithms to scan, calculate, and execute the fastest possible solution sequence, earning official Guinness World Records recognition.

The achievement required solving two distinct engineering challenges: building a mechanical system precise enough to manipulate small cube segments at high speed, and developing algorithms that could both identify the cube's current state and compute an optimal solution in milliseconds. The brothers designed and 3D-printed the four-armed mechanism themselves, with each arm capable of independent rotation.
