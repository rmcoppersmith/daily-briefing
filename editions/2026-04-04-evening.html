The scale of Waymo's expansion is striking: just two years ago, the company was operating in a handful of cities with limited hours and geofenced zones. Dubai Deploys Quadruped Robot Firefighters Active in Live Incidents

Dubai's Civil Defense has deployed quadruped robots capable of fighting fires in environments too dangerous for human firefighters — operating in temperatures up to 500°C and delivering up to 2,400 liters of water per minute. The robots are no longer in testing; they are responding to real incidents.

The Dubai robot firefighters represent a meaningful operational advance over prior robot-in-emergency deployments, which were typically limited to reconnaissance. These quadrupeds are equipped with thermal imaging, can navigate rubble and debris, and carry their own hose connections rather than requiring fixed infrastructure. The 2,400 liters-per-minute delivery rate is comparable to a small fire engine. Dubai's Civil Defense cited high-rise building fires — a persistent risk given the city's density of skyscrapers — as a primary use case. The program is being watched by fire departments in Singapore, London, and several U.S. cities exploring similar technology.
