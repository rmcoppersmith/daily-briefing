CERN Successfully Transports Antimatter on a Truck for the First Time

Researchers at CERN successfully transported 92 antiprotons on a truck with a specially designed tank that kept the antimatter from touching matter. The 30-minute spin around the campus is a first step toward an eight-hour drive to Heinrich Heine University in Düsseldorf for precision measurements.

Despite what science fiction would suggest, no danger was posed — the 92 antiprotons were so small that no damage would result from a matter collision. The big bang theory posits that equal parts matter and antimatter were created in the universe's origin; studying antimatter could explain why matter dominates. HHU won't be ready to accept the delivery until 2029 at the earliest.
