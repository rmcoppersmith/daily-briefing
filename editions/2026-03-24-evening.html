Wharton Study: 80% of People Follow Wrong AI Advice — "Cognitive Surrender" Is Real

Wharton researchers published the largest study of its kind — 1,372 people, 9,593 trials — revealing that people stop evaluating AI output and accept it as their own thinking. They call this "cognitive surrender."

The paper introduces "Tri-System Theory": System 1 is fast gut instinct, System 2 is slow deliberate reasoning, and System 3 is AI — a cognitive system outside your brain that functions as part of your mind. The problem is that System 3 replaces System 2 entirely, causing your deliberation muscle to atrophy. The researchers argue the fix is architectural: a second AI auditing the first, using structured verification protocols, because you can't willpower your way out of a brain that has already surrendered.

Simon Willison's Guide to Agentic Engineering: How Teams Should Adopt Coding Agents

Django co-creator Simon Willison published a guide distinguishing "vibe coding" from "agentic engineering" — the moment you begin reviewing and auditing agent-generated code. His sharpest warning: engineers submitting PRs full of unchecked agent code is a growing anti-pattern.

Willison argues that every working snippet and old repository serves as high-quality fuel for agents — years of knowledge compressed into a single prompt. But agents lower the cost of writing code without lowering the cost of maintaining bad code. Without updated review processes, technical debt grows with every commit. The cost shifts from the person who wrote the prompt to the team that inherits the repository.
