US and Iran Agree to Two-Week Ceasefire — Strait of Hormuz to Reopen

President Trump agreed to a 14-day ceasefire with Iran on April 7, brokered by Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif, after a day that began with threats of "annihilation" and ended with a deal. Iran's Supreme National Security Council confirmed acceptance hours later, stating it will allow safe passage through the Strait of Hormuz during the two-week window.

The ceasefire announcement came after intermediaries — led by Pakistan, with China quietly pulling strings as well — worked to head off further escalation. Trump described Iran's 10-point counterproposal (which included lifting all sanctions, withdrawing US forces from the region, reparations, and Iranian control of the Strait) as "a workable basis on which to negotiate." Formal negotiations are set to begin in Islamabad on April 11, with Vance, Witkoff, and Kushner expected. Critically, a significant narrative gap has opened: Iranian state media claims the US accepted Iran's demands including US force withdrawal, while Trump called those reports fraudulent. SitDeck flags this divergence as a "critical risk" to implementation. Iran also continued ballistic missile strikes against Israel even after the ceasefire announcement, signaling the pause applies to US-Iran hostilities only. Crude oil plunged 13.8% and the Strait's reopening is contingent on Iran following through. Markets reacted with the CNN Fear & Greed Index sitting at Extreme Fear (17), suggesting investors see the ceasefire as fragile.

Upper Middle Class Is Now America's Largest Income Group

The upper middle class — families of three earning $133k–$400k in 2024 dollars — has become the largest US income group at 31% of families, up from just 10% in 1979, according to AEI analysis. The share of Americans in or near poverty declined from 30% to below 19% over the same period.

The growth was driven primarily by dual-income professional households — white-collar workers who benefited from women's workforce gains and rising wages in fields like software engineering. Many are boomers receiving sizable pension payments from stock market gains. This group's shopping habits are behind the rise of "bougie" goods and services like first-class air travel, which have been a major US economic growth driver. However, economic inequality has also grown: higher tax brackets saw greater income gains while the poorest 5% saw earnings decline.

First Close Pair of Supermassive Black Holes Detected

Astronomers have detected the first confirmed close pair of supermassive black holes — a milestone in understanding how galaxies merge and how gravitational wave signals from merging black holes might eventually be detected.

Close supermassive black hole pairs are theoretically expected to form when galaxies collide and merge, but confirming their existence has proven difficult. The detection opens a new observational window and may point the way toward direct gravitational wave signatures at the low-frequency end of the spectrum targeted by future space-based observatories.
