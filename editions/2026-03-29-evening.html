CERN Scientists Transport Antimatter by Road for the First Time in World-First Experiment

Scientists at CERN loaded 92 antiprotons into a cryogenic trap, strapped it to a truck, and drove 6.2 miles across their Geneva campus — a feat more than ten years in the making. The breakthrough could pave the way for transporting antimatter to quieter research facilities in Germany, where conditions may help answer why matter survived the Big Bang.

Antimatter is the universe's most fragile substance, destroyed instantly upon contact with ordinary matter. This has historically confined antimatter research to the immediate vicinity of CERN's particle accelerators. The successful road transport opens the possibility of antimatter experiments at distant facilities with lower seismic and electromagnetic noise — conditions that could enable precision measurements of antimatter's gravitational behavior and test fundamental symmetries of physics.

MIT Scientists Think an Ultra-Energetic Neutrino May Be Proof of an Exploding Black Hole

In 2023, an underwater telescope off Sicily detected a neutrino orders of magnitude more energetic than anything produced in a particle collider on Earth. MIT scientists now believe it may have come from an exploding black hole — a concept Stephen Hawking predicted in 1974 but that has never been observed.

If confirmed, this would validate Hawking's theory of black hole radiation, potentially crack open the mystery of dark matter, and reveal entirely new particles. The finding was made by the KM3NeT detector, a cubic-kilometer array of light sensors suspended deep in the Mediterranean Sea, designed to capture the faint flashes produced when ultra-high-energy neutrinos collide with water molecules.

Scientists Break Key Solar Barrier With 130% Quantum Yield via Singlet Fission

A team at Kyushu University has achieved solar energy conversion exceeding 100% by triggering singlet fission — a process that splits a photon's energy into two usable charges. The experiment hit quantum yields of 130%, producing more energy carriers than absorbed photons.

The work is still proof-of-concept, but if the approach scales, it could push real-world solar panel efficiency past the limits of traditional silicon. Current commercial panels top out around 22–24% efficiency; singlet fission offers a theoretical pathway to dramatically higher ceilings by harvesting energy that conventional cells waste as heat.

World's First Beer Carbonated With CO₂ Captured From Thin Air Debuts in California

Almanac Beer Co. has launched the first beer carbonated with CO₂ pulled directly from the atmosphere. The direct-air-capture system sits inside the brewery in Alameda, California, captures ambient CO₂, and refines it to 99.999% purity for food-grade use.

The 2022 national CO₂ shortage hit breweries hard, exposing the industry's dependence on fossil-fuel-derived carbon dioxide. This approach could reduce that reliance while also serving as a proof point for atmospheric CO₂ capture technology in food production, refrigeration, agriculture, and even concrete manufacturing.

Scientists have built HOBIT, an implantable device packed with engineered cells that continuously produce multiple drugs at once — an anti-HIV antibody, a GLP-1 diabetes peptide, and an appetite-regulating hormone. In animal tests, it delivered steady doses for 30 days straight.
