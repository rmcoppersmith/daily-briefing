Meta and YouTube Found Liable in Landmark Social Media Addiction Trial

A California jury found Meta and YouTube liable for knowingly using interfaces and features that made their platforms addictive to children and teenagers, ordering $3 million in compensatory damages. The jury determined Meta bears 70% of the responsibility and YouTube 30%.

The verdict is being called a "Big Tobacco moment" for social media by child safety advocates. The landmark ruling could influence the outcome of roughly 2,000 other pending lawsuits against tech companies over social media harms to minors. While the $3 million in compensatory damages is modest, the legal precedent is significant — establishing that platform design choices that maximize engagement can constitute negligence when they harm young users.

This is separate from the $375 million New Mexico verdict against Meta from earlier this week over child predator content. Together, the two rulings represent an escalating legal reckoning for Big Tech over youth safety.
