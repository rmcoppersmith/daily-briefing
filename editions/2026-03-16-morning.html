AI-Designed Cancer Vaccine Shrinks Rescue Dog's Tumor

Sydney data engineer Paul Conyngham used ChatGPT, AlphaFold, and Grok to design a personalized mRNA cancer vaccine for his rescue dog Rosie, who was diagnosed with mast cell cancer. After the first injection in December, her tennis-ball-sized tumor shrank by 50%, and she is now chasing rabbits again.

Conyngham spent tens of thousands of dollars to sequence Rosie's tumor DNA, use AlphaFold to model mutated proteins, identify drug targets, and partner with UNSW's RNA Institute to synthesize a custom mRNA vaccine. The effort represents a powerful proof of concept for citizen science: a non-biologist outside traditional labs used widely available AI tools to solve a complex medical problem in months rather than years. UNSW researchers called it stunning and are exploring how this approach could translate to human cancer trials.

Dick's Sporting Goods Doubles Sales Over Decade

Dick's Sporting Goods has roughly doubled sales in the past 10 years, driven by the explosive growth of youth sports spending. The retailer acquired GameChanger, a score-keeping app, and is rolling out "Houses of Sport" megastores and "Fast Break" smaller concept stores to capture market share.

Youth sports have become a $40 billion annual market in the US, with average family spending jumping 46% from 2019 to 2024 and baseball spending rising nearly 70% in the same period. Dick's invested in technology through the GameChanger acquisition, which generates nearly $150M in annual revenue, and is leveraging the youth market boom with optimized store concepts and enhanced apps featuring loyalty point rewards and reservation systems.
