Meta Unveils TRIBE v2: AI Model That Predicts How the Human Brain Reacts to Stimuli

Meta's Fundamental AI Research team has released TRIBE v2, a foundation model that predicts how the human brain responds to images, audio, and video. Trained on fMRI data from over 700 volunteers watching movies and listening to podcasts, it achieves a 70x resolution increase over previous systems — and can generalize to new individuals and unseen languages without retraining.

Meta released the full model, codebase, research paper, and an interactive demo publicly. The "in-silico neuroscience" capability allows researchers to simulate neural responses to new stimuli without requiring new brain scans — dramatically reducing the cost and time of neuroscience research. Potential applications span brain-computer interfaces, neurological disorder treatment, and using brain insights to improve AI architectures themselves. The move continues Meta's pattern of releasing foundational research as open infrastructure.
